# Psychology Concept Index

All named psychology concepts extracted across the C.L.E.A.R. framework, with academic sources and the pillar(s) where they appear.

## Attention & Cognition

| Concept | Source | Pillar(s) | Definition |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Cognitive Miser** | Fiske & Taylor, 1991 | C | People default to the least effortful mental path. Every extra word, element, or choice is a tax. |
| **Cognitive Load** | Wagemans et al., 2012 | L | Clutter forces re-interpretation. Layout reduces load by making structure predictable. |
| **Chunking** | Miller, 1956 | L | Breaking information into groups of 5±2 items makes it parseable. |
| **Preattentive Features** | Treisman & Gelade, 1980 | E | Visual properties processed before conscious attention: color, size, orientation, motion. The neurological substrate of the six emphasis dials. |

## Memory & Perception

| Concept | Source | Pillar(s) | Definition |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Von Restorff Effect** | Hedwig von Restorff, 1933 | E | An item that stands out from its surroundings is more likely to be remembered. The foundation of emphasis. |
| **Law of Prägnanz** | Gestalt psychology | L | The brain prefers the simplest possible interpretation. Simpler layouts feel more trustworthy. |
| **Gestalt Principles** | Wertheimer, 1923 / Koffka, 1935 | L | The brain groups visual elements by proximity, similarity, continuity, closure, common region, and simplicity. |
| **Pattern-Break Tax** | UX research | L | Every visual inconsistency forces a micro re-evaluation: "Is this intentional?" Each one is a cognitive cost. |
| **Retrieval Cues** | Cognitive psychology | E | Emphasis elements serve as memory anchors. The big teal number becomes "I remember that dashboard." |

## Motivation & Behavior

| Concept | Source | Pillar(s) | Definition |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Reactance** | Brehm, 1966 | E, A | When people feel their freedom is threatened, they resist. Screaming UIs trigger: "Don't tell me where to look." |
| **Post-Purchase Dissonance** | Consumer psychology | R | Buyer's remorse after a commitment. Immediate reward copy reduces it. |
| **Selfish Design** | growth.design | R | When the reward serves the business, not the user. Users detect this and disengage. |
| **Alignment of Incentives** | Behavioral economics | R | The best rewards align user goals and business goals. Sustainable engagement. |

## Social & Identity

| Concept | Source | Pillar(s) | Definition |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Identifiable Person Effect** | J. Behavioral Science, 2005; Oxford Academic, 2014 | L | People engage more when a person is identifiable (name, photo, story) vs. abstract (IDs, numbers). |
| **Social Presence** | Social psychology | E | Emphasizing human elements (faces, names) leverages the identifiable person effect in UI. |
| **WIIFM** | Marketing / UX | C | "What's In It For Me" — the implicit question every user asks of every screen element. |
| **Voice of Customer (VoC)** | Market research | C | Using the user's own words mirrors their mental model, reducing cognitive translation cost. |

## Design-Specific

| Concept | Source | Pillar(s) | Definition |
|---------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Feedforward** | Vermeulen et al., 2013 | C, R | A CTA that previews its outcome. "Start free trial" is feedforward; "Submit" is not. |
| **Proactive Reassurance** | UX copywriting | C, R | Addressing user objections before they surface. "No credit card required." |
| **Aesthetic Usability Effect** | NN Group | All | Users perceive aesthetically pleasing designs as more usable than they objectively are. |
| **Progressive Disclosure** | IxD, NN/g, 2025 | L | Show essential info first, reveal details on demand. Collapsible sections = table of contents. |
| **Discoverability Debt** | UX research | A | Novel interactions without onboarding create invisible functionality. If users can't find it, it doesn't exist. |
| **Price Anchoring** | Behavioral economics | C, E | Showing a higher price first makes the actual price feel like a deal. UI tool for pricing screens. |
| **Reward Flywheel** | growth.design | R | Good reward → more engagement → more data → better personalization → better reward. Virtuous cycle. |
| **Time as Design Ally** | growth.design (closing principle) | All | Small improvements compound. 1-point scorecard improvement per sprint transforms a product. |
