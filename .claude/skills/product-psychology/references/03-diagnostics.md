# Diagnostic Question Bank

Organized by skill. Each set of questions is designed to be asked about a specific product, feature, or screen to identify failures and opportunities.

---

## Skill 1: 6P Story Diagnostics

1. Can you describe your target user as one specific person — not a segment, not a persona, not "users"?
2. What does this person hope to achieve? (Not what your product does for them — what THEY want.)
3. What's their biggest frustration right now — in their words, not yours?
4. What have they already tried to solve this? Why did those attempts fail?
5. If your product disappeared, what would they do instead?
6. Can you tell their story in under 60 seconds and make a stranger care?

## Skill 2: BMAP Diagnostics

1. What specific behavior are we trying to trigger? (Be precise — "use the product" is not a behavior.)
2. Which motivation dyad is dominant: Sensation, Anticipation, or Social?
3. Which of the five ability constraints is the weakest link: Time, Money, Physical Effort, Mental Capacity, or Social Deviation?
4. Where does this behavior land on the BMAP graph — Zone A, B, C, or D?
5. Is the prompt arriving when Motivation and Ability are already above the threshold?
6. Are we solving an Ability problem with a Motivation solution (or vice versa)?
7. Could subconscious filters (banner blindness, pattern-matching) be blocking the prompt?

## Skill 3: NPV Diagnostics

1. List every touchpoint in the flow. Now score each from -5 to +5.
2. Where does the cumulative score first go negative?
3. Are there three or more consecutive -1 touchpoints? (Compound friction risk.)
4. Is there at least one moment above +3? (Peak requirement.)
5. What is the final touchpoint's score? (End-of-experience memory.)
6. Are we adding motivation or removing friction? Which would move the needle more?
7. Is there any friction that's actually *good* (commitment-building, trust-signaling, quality-filtering)?

## Skill 4: Block Diagnostics

1. What is the user's primary task on this screen?
2. How many elements are competing for attention? Which one should win?
3. Is the critical element in a position the user has learned to ignore (banner, edge, popup)?
4. Would a first-time user know where to look?
5. Is there a pattern break that could recapture attention — without becoming another pattern?
6. What is the DPP (Decisions Per Page)? Is it above 3–4?

## Skill 5: Interpret Diagnostics

1. Read every piece of copy on this screen. After each, ask "So what?" from the user's perspective.
2. Are we describing features or benefits?
3. What's the first number or reference point the user sees? What expectation does it set?
4. Is loss framing appropriate at this investment level, or does it feel like nagging?
5. Can the user discover the most important action without instructions?
6. If there's a loading state, does it show work being done?
7. Would this make sense if the user had never seen the product before?

## Skill 6: Act Diagnostics

1. What is the single most important action on this screen?
2. How many decisions must the user make before taking that action? (DPP count)
3. Is there a valid default that 80% of informed users would choose?
4. Could we break this into progressive steps without adding total effort?
5. Is there social proof? Is it specific and verifiable?
6. Is there an escape hatch? ("Skip" or "Not now" that preserves autonomy)
7. Are we using scarcity or urgency? Is it real or manufactured?
8. Could any element trigger reactance (feeling of forced action)?

## Skill 7: Store Diagnostics

1. After completing the action, does the user know it worked? (Clear feedback)
2. Is there proactive reassurance for the anxiety that follows commitment?
3. Is there at least one unexpected moment of delight?
4. What will the user remember about this experience tomorrow? (Peak-End check)
5. If the user doesn't return for a week, what pulls them back?
6. Have our delighters become expected? Are they due for rotation?
7. Does the experience end on a high note, or does it trail off?

## Skill 8: Journey Map Diagnostics

1. Have you identified exactly 5–6 emotionally pivotal moments? (Not all touchpoints — only the ones that move the needle.)
2. Which moment is the peak? Is it strong enough to be remembered?
3. Which moment is the biggest pit? What makes it painful?
4. How does the experience end? (Peak-End Rule: this moment disproportionately affects memory.)
5. Are there consecutive drops without a jump between them? (Compound fatigue risk.)
6. Is there a pit followed by a peak? Could they be reordered?
7. Are there any transitions that could be shortened without losing value?
8. Does the journey's shape look like a story (6P arc) or a flat line?

## Skill 9: Journey Improvement Diagnostics

1. Can the biggest pit be eliminated entirely, or only softened?
2. Can we reorder to put a peak before the biggest pit?
3. Is there a transition that could be shortened by 50%+ without losing value?
4. Are we adding a feature, or can we fix this by changing the sequence?
5. Is hyperbolic discounting working for us (reward first) or against us (cost first)?
6. After reordering, do we still have the Peak-End Rule covered?

## Skill 10: Communicate Diagnostics

1. Who needs to approve this? What KPI do they care about most?
2. Are you presenting a solution or presenting the problem first?
3. Is the stakeholder's concern about the design, or about something else (timeline, resource, risk)?
4. Can you translate your design reasoning into their language?
5. Are you offering a recommendation, or presenting too many options?
6. Have you anticipated the likely pushback and prepared a response?

## Skill 11: Ethics Diagnostics

1. **Regret Test:** If the user fully understood this mechanism, would they still make the same choice?
2. **Maker Test:** Would you use this product yourself, the way users use it?
3. **Scale Test:** If a billion people experienced this for ten years, what breaks?
4. Is this nudge helping users do what they want, or making them do what we want?
5. Is our loss framing based on a real loss, or are we manufacturing fear?
6. Do our defaults serve users or our conversion goals?
7. Could our social proof be misleading or unverifiable?
8. Is the user's time being respected, or are we optimizing for our engagement metrics?

## Skill 12: Iterate Diagnostics

1. Which B.I.A.S. phase did our change target?
2. Did the intended metric move? By how much?
3. Did any adjacent metric move unexpectedly?
4. Was there an upstream failure we skipped? (Sequencing Rule check)
5. If the metric didn't move, did we fix the wrong level?
6. Are we seeing a V2 dip? Is the team interpreting it correctly?
7. What new data does this result give us for the next empathy cycle?
8. Have any Kano classifications shifted since our last check?
