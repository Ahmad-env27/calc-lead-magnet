## Full Worked Cases

### Airbnb "Snow White" Project
**Skill:** 1 (6P Story)
**Context:** Airbnb used storyboarding (the 6P narrative structure) to shift from data-driven to story-driven product development. Named after Disney's storytelling methodology.
**Insight:** The stories revealed emotional pain points (anxiety about staying in a stranger's home) that survey data had captured as "trust concerns" — a much less actionable framing. The narrative format made the user's experience viscerable to the entire team.

### DoorDash DashPass Subscription Upsell (V0 → V3)
**Skills:** 4–7 (full B.I.A.S. audit), 12 (Iterate)
**Context:** Four iterations of the DashPass subscription prompt in the DoorDash gifting flow, each targeting a different B.I.A.S. phase.
**Key pattern:** V0 had Block failures (subscription prompt invisible in the flow). V1 fixed Block but created Interpret failures (unclear value proposition). V2 fixed Interpret but revealed Act friction (too many steps to subscribe). V3 fixed Act. Each version showed the Sequencing Rule in action — and V2 showed the expected dip.

### ActiveCampaign Email Campaign (Pit → Peak Reorder)
**Skills:** 8–9 (Journey Map, Improve)
**Context:** Users sending their first email campaign had to configure complex settings (pit) before seeing their email go out (peak). Reordering to show a quick-win preview first (peak) before asking for configuration (pit, now tolerable) transformed the experience.
**Key principle:** Hyperbolic discounting — the same reward experienced sooner is worth more than the same reward experienced later. The features didn't change. The sequence changed.

### Brave Browser Onboarding
**Skills:** 8–9 (Journey Map, Improve)
**Context:** Practice case for journey mapping. The onboarding flow had a long transition (downloading, installing) followed by a pit (unfamiliar interface) before reaching any peak (faster browsing, ad blocking).
**Diagnosis:** The peak was real but delayed. Inserting a micro-peak (showing blocked tracker count immediately) shortened the path to reward.

## Before/After Contrasts

### U-Haul Checkout
**Skill:** 5 (Interpret)
**Before:** Dense, cluttered checkout page with competing information hierarchies. Users couldn't distinguish required fields from optional upsells. Cognitive load was extreme — everything looked equally important.
**After:** Clear visual hierarchy, required fields separated from optional add-ons, benefit framing on insurance options ("Protect your belongings" instead of "Add SafeMove coverage"), and progressive disclosure of complex options.
**Principles demonstrated:** Cognitive Load, Clear Benefits, Discoverability, Progressive Disclosure.

### Spotify/Growth.Design Activation (V0 → V3 Iteration)
**Skill:** 2 (BMAP), 12 (Iterate)
**V0 (7% activation):** Below the activation threshold — low Ability (unfamiliar interface) and no prompt timing.
**V1:** Added defaults (removed Mental Capacity cost). Moved above threshold. Activation rose.
**V2 (dip):** Fixed Interpret (clearer onboarding) but surfaced hidden friction. Temporary dip.
**V3 (85% activation):** Fixed the newly-visible friction. Breakthrough.
**Key learning:** The V2 dip is the canonical example of the Sequencing Rule in action.

### Fastic Onboarding
**Skills:** 5–6 (Interpret, Act)
**Before:** Onboarding flow that front-loaded information (features, science, methodology) before letting the user do anything. High cognitive load, no early reward.
**After:** Progressive disclosure — minimum viable information, then immediate action (choose a fasting plan), then deeper information as the user requests it.
**Principles:** Labor Illusion (unnamed in the source but doing active work — the fasting timer showing progress creates perceived value), Progressive Disclosure, Hick's Law.

## Illustrative Examples

### Uber Eats Notification Timing
**Skill:** 2 (BMAP)
**Example:** Sending food notifications at lunch/dinner time (when hunger activates Sensation lever, pushing Motivation above Activation Threshold) vs. 9 AM (low Motivation, high Ability = Zone D).

### Kijiji Seller Experience
**Skill:** 7 (Store)
**Example:** After listing an item, the seller sees "Your listing is live!" with view count. This is a Clear Feedback storer. Without it, the seller doesn't know if the listing worked, creating anxiety (negative Store).

### Amazon One-Click
**Skill:** 6 (Act)
**Example:** The canonical reduction of DPP to 1. One decision: buy or don't. Every other decision (shipping, payment, address) is handled by valid defaults.

### Readwise Daily Review
**Skill:** 7 (Store)
**Example:** Surfaces highlights from books the user has already read. Leverages Baader-Meinhof (re-encountering ideas makes them feel more relevant) and creates a Feeling of Caring storer (the product remembers what you read).

### Lumosity Session Complete
**Skill:** 7 (Store)
**Example:** Post-session screen showing performance relative to past sessions. Clear Feedback (did I improve?) + Social Proof (percentile ranking) + Delighter (animated celebration at new personal bests).
