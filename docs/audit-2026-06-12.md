# Calculator Conversion Audit — 12 Jun 2026

Audit of the current build against the 10 Jun team-sync brief, run with the
`clear-ui-framework` and `product-psychology` skills. Findings only — no
changes made. Ordered by impact on conversion and credibility, not effort.

---

## Executive summary — the five findings that matter

1. **The email ask is in the wrong place (biggest pit in the journey).**
   Email + brand name are collected at Step 1 — the moment of lowest trust and
   lowest investment. The agreed decision was "email unlock before report":
   the ask belongs *after* the quiz, when sunk cost and curiosity peak ("Your
   score for [brand] is ready — where should we send it?"). This is the classic
   Pit→Peak reorder (Journey Improve, Skill 9) and the single highest-leverage
   change for completion rate. Cold Instagram traffic that bounces on an email
   field at Step 1 never even becomes a measurable lead.

2. **The sweet-spot lead sees a number that undercuts the fee.** Tested with
   the real scoring code: a £30–50k-spend, low-diversity lead who skips the
   optional ROAS field gets **£2,765–£5,135/mo** — at or below the £5k/mo fee
   Ahmad needs it to anchor. With ROAS provided it jumps to £6.3–11.6k (works).
   The fix is structural: make ROAS a required multiple-choice bracket in the
   main flow, which also helps finding 3.

3. **The quiz is 8 questions; the agreed minimum is 12.** And the frustration
   list is 9 options vs the agreed 10 + disqualifier. Adding 3–4 multiple-choice
   questions (ROAS bracket, AOV bracket, new-creatives-per-month, who-makes-
   your-ads) simultaneously fixes volume (Josh #3), the fee anchor (Ahmad B),
   formula guardrails (Josh #4), and the "earned output" feeling — one change,
   four findings.

4. **The results page frames loss where Josh asked for opportunity.** The
   payoff card is titled "ESTIMATED MONTHLY REVENUE LEAK" on a red-tinted
   card — pure waste-framing. Josh's #1 point: flip to "what you could be
   making." Loss-tease on the *landing page* should stay (ad congruence, low
   investment = curiosity), but at the results stage the user has invested —
   the reveal should read as discovered upside, amber/gold not red.

5. **The Loom CTA — the conversion point — is the 5th section, below the
   fold.** Ahmad's agreed hierarchy is number → teardown promise → CTA →
   proof. Currently: number → interpretation → 3 angle cards → quick win →
   Loom. Two tinted cards (red leak, green quick-win) outcompete it before it
   appears. And the settled "inline booking in the same attention span" is not
   implemented at all (claim button → email promise only).

## Product-psychology pass

**Journey map (Psych Level, 6 pivotal moments):**

| # | Moment | Score | Type |
|---|--------|-------|------|
| 1 | Ad click → landing promise | +2 | Jump |
| 2 | **Step 1 email ask** | **−3** | **Pit — at the worst position (before any investment)** |
| 3 | Steps 2–7 tapping | ~0 | Flat transition (no deposits, no acknowledgment) |
| 4 | Loading tension | +1 | Transition (good) |
| 5 | **Gauge + number reveal** | **+4** | **Peak (correctly the strongest moment)** |
| 6 | Post-reveal: wants to act → CTA below fold, no booking | −2 | Drop at the conversion moment |

The shape violates the reorder matrix twice: Pit→(long flat)→Peak (user can
quit before the peak), and Peak→Drop ("bait and switch" feel for hot leads).
Fixes: move the email pit to just before the peak (unlock screen), and put the
Loom CTA inside the peak's attention span.

**BMAP:** cold IG traffic sits at moderate Motivation / high Ability *only if
the flow stays tap-only*. The Step 1 typed email ask arrives when Motivation
is lowest — below the activation threshold for a chunk of traffic. Same
prompt after the loading tease lands when Motivation is at its maximum.

**B.I.A.S. sequencing for the fix order:** reframe copy first (Interpret),
then CTA prominence (Act), then booking inline (Act), then measure. Expect a
possible V2-dip when the email moves — completion of *quiz starts* will rise
while email capture rate per start may look worse before net leads improve.
Don't panic-revert.

**Ethics check (Manipulation Matrix):** clean. No manufactured scarcity, the
formula admits its assumptions, the >8x-ROAS honesty note exists. One caution
on Ahmad B ("inflate within reason"): inflate through *defensible levers*
(65% skincare margins, 2.87x average ROAS, the 8–12 assets/month benchmark)
rather than a bare multiplier — Josh's own worry (#4) is being unable to
defend the number on the call. The Regret Test passes only if the strategist
can show the working live.

## Formula stress test (run against the real `scoring.js`)

| Case | Score | Monthly output | Verdict |
|------|-------|----------------|---------|
| £10k spend, 5x ROAS, decent practice | 40 | £490–£910 | ⚠️ Displayed low (£490) dips *below* the £700 floor — floor is applied before the ±30% spread. Reads as noise |
| £10k spend, 20x ROAS (suspect input) | 59 | £851–£1,580 + "non-paid channels" note | ✓ Handled |
| £100k spend, 2x ROAS, poor practice | 75 | £30.8k–£57.3k | ⚠️ Ambitious-to-implausible: up to 29% of their total paid revenue. Borderline |
| £125k spend, 1x ROAS, worst practice | 79 | **£50k–£93k (£0.6–1.1M/yr)** | ❌ Fantasy. No cap. This is the "embarrassing and destroys trust" case |
| £50k spend, 3.5x ROAS, healthy brand | 37 | £1.6k–£2.9k | ⚠️ "Moderate risk" label + leak number mildly contradicts a healthy brand — spend-weight pushes big healthy spenders out of the "low" band |
| **Sweet spot, no ROAS given (typical)** | 70 | **£2.8k–£5.1k** | ❌ **Fails the fee anchor** (target £8–25k) |
| Sweet spot, ROAS 2.4 given | 70 | £6.3k–£11.6k | ✓ Works — proves ROAS must be a required question |

**Structural gap:** the revenue tier (Step 3) is collected but *never used*
in the formula. It's the natural guardrail: cap recoverable at a plausible
share of revenue-tier midpoint, and flag implausible spend/revenue combos.
All four of Josh's edge cases are revenue-relative — the formula can't see
them today.

## Prioritised fix list (impact order, not effort)

1. **Move email (+ keep brand name early) to an unlock screen after the quiz** — fixes the biggest pit, satisfies settled #7 as worded, adds an emotional beat (Josh #2). The unlock screen *is* the tension moment: "We've built your report for [brand] — where should we send it?"
2. **Make ROAS a required multiple-choice bracket; add 3 more MC questions; extend frustrations to 10 + disqualifier** — fixes Josh #3, Ahmad B, settled #3/#5, and feeds guardrails.
3. **Recalibrate the formula:** use the revenue tier as a cap (recoverable ≤ ~plausible share of revenue midpoint), apply the £700 floor *after* the ±30% spread, soften the spend-weight for healthy-practice brands, ground the uplift in the industry data (margins, asset-velocity benchmark) so every number is defensible on the call.
4. **Reframe the results payoff as opportunity** — "What [brand] could be adding each month," amber/gold card, keep the working shown. Kill "creative velocity," fix "DTC," soften the critical-band copy.
5. **Restructure results hierarchy:** number → Loom promise (with the two held-back angles named and locked) → CTA → angles/quick win as supporting evidence → second CTA → formula footer. Add the GHL calendar embed/modal on claim.
6. **Small Reward injections in the quiz:** one acknowledgment beat after the frustrations step, loading messages that reference their actual answers.
7. **Pixel PageView decision** — confirm with Josh whether the base PageView stays (recommended) or strict after-qualification-only firing wins.
8. Accessibility touch-ups: bump `--faint` small-text contrast, label why chips disable at max.

**Sequencing note (Upstream Principle):** do 1–3 (structure + formula) before
4–5 (copy + emphasis polish). Reframing copy on top of a formula that outputs
£2.8k for the sweet-spot lead polishes the wrong layer. Expect a V2-style dip
in per-start email rate when the unlock moves — measure net leads per ad
pound, not step conversion in isolation.
